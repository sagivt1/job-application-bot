# What to write
- Identify the candidate's genuine strengths from the CV and map them to the
  requirements that THIS specific JD actually emphasizes. Do not assume a seniority
  level or a fixed set of skills — let the CV and JD decide what to highlight.
- Translate technical work into business value a recruiter cares about: building
  reliable systems, shipping them successfully to production, and working well in
  agile teams — but only where the CV supports those claims.

# Format (follow exactly)
- A brief, warm opening hook (no generic filler such as "I am writing to express my
  interest in...").
- Exactly THREE short bullet points, each starting with "• ", that tie one concrete
  CV strength to one key requirement from the JD.
- A quick closing that confidently proposes a brief introductory phone call.
- Sign off with the candidate's name from the CV.
- Plain text only — no markdown headings or bold, no tables. Strictly UNDER 250
  words total.

# Tone
Warm, professional, straightforward, and energetic. Confident without arrogance.
